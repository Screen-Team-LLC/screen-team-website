Window Screen Repair & Replacement

Window screens in Florida face more abuse than most — intense UV exposure breaks down the mesh, heavy rain and wind push against the frames, and the humidity that comes with living near the coast accelerates deterioration. Add in the occasional storm or wayward branch and it's easy to end up with torn, bent, or missing screens.

The Screen Team handles window screen repairs and custom replacements for homes throughout Tampa Bay. Whether it's a single screen or every window in the house, Chris brings the right materials and does the job cleanly.

What We Do

  • Torn or ripped screen mesh replacement
  • Warped or bent frame repair and replacement
  • Re-splinning screens that have come loose from the frame
  • Custom-cut screens to fit non-standard window sizes
  • Fiberglass and aluminum mesh options
  • No-see-um fine mesh for smaller insects

When to Replace vs. Repair

If only the mesh is damaged and the frame is still straight and solid, a re-screen is the cost-effective fix. If the frame itself is bent, corroded, or no longer holds its shape, a full frame and mesh replacement makes more sense. Chris will tell you honestly which is the right call — no upsell for the sake of a bigger ticket.

Window Screen FAQs

Answers about window screen repair and replacement in Tampa Bay.

Yes. Screen frames are cut and assembled to fit your specific window openings, so non-standard or older window sizes aren't a problem. We measure accurately before building replacements.

Frames with minor warping can sometimes be re-straightened. Badly bent, cracked, or corroded frames are better replaced — a damaged frame won't hold the screen material properly or seal correctly in the window opening. Chris will evaluate on-site.

Standard fiberglass screen provides insect protection while allowing airflow. Solar screens reduce UV and heat gain — useful on south- or west-facing windows. No-see-um screens use a finer mesh to block tiny biting insects. The right choice depends on your exposure and priorities.

Standard window screens last 5–10 years in Florida conditions with normal use. Brittleness, tears, or mesh pulling away from the frame are the main signs it's time for replacement.
